## Confirmed by Convergent Evidence

### Somatic Intelligence Domain (S1.1–S1.4)
**Support: Strong.**
- 8-week MBSR increases right insula cortical thickness and somatosensory cortex thickness, with concurrent decrease in alexithymia (P2, n=23). This is the strongest direct neuroanatomical confirmation of somatic-emotion mapping (E1.2) as a trainable capacity with measurable structural correlate.
- The salience network (anterior insula + ACC) shows specifically improved functional reconfiguration efficiency after 8-week mindfulness therapy, not after active sleep hygiene control (P6). This is the functional-connectivity equivalent of interoceptive accuracy improvement.
- **Timescale:** Structural changes (cortical thickness) demonstrated at 8 weeks. Functional reconfiguration changes also at 8 weeks.
- **Critical qualification:** Focused-attention breath meditation does NOT appear to train somatic awareness independently — the SMS-Body subscale showed no significant group difference in P1 (fMRI NF study targeting PCC). Somatic Intelligence may require dedicated practices (body scan, somatic tracking) rather than breath-based focused attention.

### Emotional Literacy Domain (E1.1–E1.4)
**Support: Strong for E1.2 (somatic-emotion mapping). Moderate for E1.4 (gradient detection).**
- Insula thickness increase + alexithymia decrease from MBSR (P2) is direct evidence for somatic-emotion mapping as trainable.
- Amygdala reactivity decrease after 8-week MBSR (P3) supports emotional regulation at the level of automatic stimulus processing, which precedes explicit labeling — relevant to E1.4 (emotional gradient detection as an early-warning system).
- MBCT mechanism explicitly trains decentering from emotional-cognitive cascade patterns, which operationally requires emotional gradient detection (E1.4) to interrupt early.

### Attention Domain (A1.1–A1.5)
**Support: Neural efficiency confirmed. Behavioral test performance NOT consistently confirmed.**
- ECN functional reconfiguration efficiency increases significantly after 8-week mindfulness (P6) — less neural effort required to enter focused task state. This is the neural substrate of practiced attentional control.
- PCC-DLPFC coupling (mind-wandering suppression + executive control engagement) increases with 2-day NF-guided meditation (P1), confirming the neural architecture of attention directing and distraction resistance.
- However: BCT accuracy declined in the group showing strongest neural and subjective improvement (P1). Multiple reviews report that neuropsychological attention tests do NOT consistently improve with MBSR/MBCT (P2, P3). Lao et al. 2016 (cited in P3): "short-term mindfulness did not enhance theorized attentional pathways" on objective tests.
- **Critical qualification added to taxonomy:** Neural efficiency gains (ECN reconfiguration, PCC-DLPFC coupling) are supported markers of attentional development. Behavioral test scores (BCT accuracy, neuropsychological measures) are unreliable proxies — do not use as primary outcome metrics.
- **Timescale:** Neural reconfiguration effects at 8 weeks. Whether these precede or follow subjective attentional improvement is unclear; P6 found neural and behavioral change were NOT correlated within the same measurement window.

### Thought Observation / Narrative Monitoring (C2.1, C2.6)
**Support: Strong. This is the best-evidenced Layer 2 skill.**
- SMS-Mind subscale (mindful awareness of thinking / mental events) increased specifically in the veridical NF group (P1), not sham. The trained mechanism was intentional disengagement from self-referential thought — operational definition of narrative monitoring.
- MBCT's evidence base (P3, 87-study SR) rests fundamentally on teaching practitioners to observe negative thought patterns as events rather than facts. The Kuyken et al. 60-week depression relapse prevention data represents the strongest outcome evidence for pattern work (P3.1–P3.3) in this corpus.
- PCC deactivation during meditation (P1) is the neural signature of the default mode network stepping back from self-narrative — the network-level correlate of thought observation.

### Meta-Attention / Dual Presence (A2.1, A2.2)
**Support: Strong.**
- FFMQ Observing subscale (noticing internal experiences, thoughts, emotions) increased specifically in the mindfulness group, not the active control group (P6). This is the closest self-report measure to meta-attention.
- Salience network reconfiguration efficiency improved specifically in the mindfulness group (P6) — the salience network is the neural substrate for interoceptive awareness and switching between internal and external attention. This is the neural basis of dual presence.
- PCC-DLPFC coupling is the neuroimaging correlate of meta-awareness during meditation: executive monitoring running concurrently with object-level focus (P1).

### State Shifting (M3.2)
**Support: Strong at neural level.**
- Rest-to-task FC reconfiguration efficiency is a direct neural measure of state-shifting ease (P6). Mindfulness training significantly improved this across three networks (ECN, DMN, SN); the active sleep control group showed declines.
- Alpha/theta EEG during mindfulness practice corresponds to a distinct "relaxed alertness" state (P3), distinguishable from ordinary rest and task states — confirming state shifting as a real and measurable phenomenon.
- Stress (SMD 0.57), anxiety (SMD 0.57), depression (SMD 0.48) reductions across large literature (P3) support arousal calibration and voluntary relaxation claims — with appropriate inflation caveat.

### Directed Neuroplastic Practice (P3.5)
**Support: Strong in principle. Conditions are critical.**
- Hippocampal volume increase after 8-week MBSR (P2), insula thickness increase (P2), PCC-DLPFC coupling changes (P1), FC reconfiguration changes (P6) — all represent measurable structural/functional neuroplastic change from deliberate practice.
- Cochlear implant and visual prosthetics literature (P4, P5) provide the mechanistic framework: directed, behaviorally relevant training can reshape cortical organization in adults, but only when the input is behaviorally meaningful (not just any repetitive exposure).
- Gamification-enhanced perceptual learning effects persist up to 2 years (P4, P5) when attentional neuromodulators (dopamine, acetylcholine) are recruited through engaging contexts.

## Where the Taxonomy Required Revision

### R1: BCT is not a valid attentional skill proxy
**Sources: P1, P6**
BCT accuracy declined in the group with strongest neural and subjective improvement (P1), interpreted as increased decentering awareness reducing mechanical counting accuracy. P6 used BCT as a task condition, not an outcome measure. The taxonomy must not treat BCT-style performance as an unambiguous marker of attentional development. Neural efficiency measures (ECN reconfiguration) are preferred.

### R2: Objective attention tests are unreliable at MBI timescales
**Sources: P2, P3 (Lao et al.), P5**
Multiple reviews find that standard neuropsychological attention tests do not consistently improve after 8-week MBSR/MBCT. Neural-level improvements precede (or run parallel to) behavioral test improvements. This creates an assessment challenge for the taxonomy's Layer 1 Attention domain.
**Resolution:** Taxonomy assessment design should use neural efficiency proxies or ecologically valid measures rather than standard cognitive tests where possible.

### R3: Focused attention meditation does not reliably develop Somatic Intelligence
**Source: P1**
SMS-Body (somatic awareness subscale) showed no significant group difference in the fMRI NF study; only SMS-Mind improved. This suggests that PCC-targeted focused attention practice selectively develops cognitive/narrative awareness, not body-level somatic awareness.
**Resolution:** Somatic Intelligence sub-skills (S1.1–S1.4) require dedicated somatic practices (body scan, somatic tracking, interoceptive attention exercises) in addition to or instead of standard breath-focused meditation.

### R4: Impulsivity does not improve with standard mindfulness
**Sources: P2 (Korponay et al.), P3**
8-week mindfulness does not reduce behavioral impulsivity in naive practitioners on objective neuropsychological tests. This constrains what the taxonomy can claim for pattern interruption (P3.2) within standard MBI timeframes.
**Resolution:** P3.2 (Pattern Interruption) requires deliberate, targeted training formats (not general mindfulness). Standard MBI protocols are insufficient.

### R5: Brain change and subjective change are decoupled
**Source: P6**
Neural reconfiguration improvements and mindfulness score improvements were NOT significantly correlated within the same 8-week measurement window (all p>0.05 from 1000 permutations). The taxonomy's implicit assumption that neural change and subjective experience change are tightly coupled is not supported within typical assessment windows.
**Resolution:** The progression model (next document) must use multiple independent measurement tracks — neural proxies and subjective measures — rather than treating one as an indicator of the other.

### R6: Individual plasticity variation is large and uncharacterized
**Sources: P4, P5**
Up to 80% of outcome variance in cochlear implant literature is unaccounted for by known hardware/software factors; "wetware" differences are the major variable. Individual differences in neural plasticity capacity are substantial and poorly characterized.
**Resolution:** Added I1.4 (Practice Quality Monitoring) as a cross-layer assessment concept.

## New Concepts Added to Taxonomy (v0.2)

### I1.4: Practice Quality Monitoring (new cross-layer skill)
**Rationale:** P1, P4, and P5 converge on the finding that behavioral relevance and practice quality — not mere repetition — determine whether neuroplastic change occurs.
- P4: "Not just any input into the wetware will result in appropriate changes: the input must contain behaviorally important information."
- P5: Generalization of learning promoted by task variability, appropriate difficulty level, and graded (not binary) feedback.
- P1: Real-time feedback loops (neurofeedback) accelerate learning by providing immediate, relevant signal.
**Skill definition:** The ability to assess and sustain the quality conditions that make practice neuroplastically effective: behavioral relevance, appropriate challenge level, contextual variety, and intentional engagement. Absence of this skill means practice quantity substitutes for quality without producing structural change.

### Timescale Differentiation (annotation added, not a new skill)
Neural and behavioral changes operate on different timescales within the same training period. The convergent evidence suggests:
- **Early (weeks 1–4):** Subjective experience changes, self-report improvements, reduced distress. May not reflect structural change yet.
- **Mid (weeks 4–8):** Functional connectivity reconfiguration, network efficiency gains. May precede or be independent of self-report changes.
- **Late (weeks 8+):** Structural changes (cortical thickness, hippocampal volume). Require sustained, consistent practice over months.
- **Very long-term:** Advanced meditators show sustained and increasing (not habituating) prefrontal activation (P2, Davanger et al.), suggesting continued development in expert practitioners.

## Publication Bias Acknowledgment
**Source: P3 (Coronado-Montoya et al.)**
87% of MBT RCTs published positive results. This implies systematic inflation of reported effect sizes across the entire literature base. The taxonomy should treat SMD values from MBI reviews as upper estimates.

## Papers That Are Analogical Only (Limited Direct Implication)
- **P4 (cochlear implant):** Validates adult plasticity mechanisms and behavioral relevance gate. Does not provide direct psychological skill training evidence.
- **P5 (visual prosthetics):** Validates plasticity hierarchy (higher-order representations more malleable). Reinforces specificity-without-variability risk for training design.
- **P7 (cortical visual prosthesis, n=2):** Conceptually validates the principle that subjective experience reliably correlates with neural state (R=0.71–0.87), supporting calibration honesty. No direct training implications; n=2 precludes any statistical generalization.
