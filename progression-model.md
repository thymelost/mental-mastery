# Mental Mastery — Progression Model

**Version:** 0.2
**Updated:** 2026-03-02 — Added stress-transfer constraint to L2→L3 gate and I1.1 thresholds (Raio 2013); added metacognitive domain-specificity design principle (Morales 2018, Fleming 2014); added protection-not-enhancement principle (Jha 2015–2020); added two new failure modes; physiological arousal added to Track B.
**Depends on:** `skill-taxonomy.md` v0.3
**Purpose:** Define measurable thresholds, phase structure, gate criteria, assessment methods, and failure modes for developing the 41 skills in the taxonomy.

---

## Design Principles

1. **Entry point is individual.** Everyone begins at a different point in Layer 1. No one skips Phase 0.
2. **Two independent measurement tracks.** Track A (subjective/behavioral) and Track B (physiological/performance) must advance independently. Progress on one does not confirm progress on the other.
3. **BCT accuracy and standard neuropsychological tests are not valid progress markers** for the Attention domain. Neural efficiency proxies and ecological measures are preferred.
4. **Layer 3 requires demonstrated Layer 2 stability, not just completion.** The L2→L3 gate is the highest-stakes boundary in the framework. Premature entry is the primary catastrophic failure mode.
5. **All skill thresholds are functional, not ideal.** "Sufficient" means reliable under normal conditions and meaningful degradation under load — not perfection.
6. **Somatic skills require dedicated somatic practices.** Breath-focused meditation alone does not develop Domain 1.
7. **Publication bias caveat.** Effect size estimates from mindfulness literature are likely inflated (87% positive publication rate). Thresholds here are conservative.
8. **Metacognitive training is domain-specific, not domain-general.** Training attention monitoring does not transfer to emotion monitoring or boundary monitoring. Each Layer 2 domain must be trained independently. A practitioner who can accurately observe thoughts may still be blind to contagion dynamics or self-other signal confusion. Do not infer cross-domain metacognitive competence from within-domain performance (Morales et al., 2018; Shekhar & Lau, 2018; Fleming, 2014).
9. **Training provides stress-resistance, not enhancement under stress.** Jha et al. (2015–2020) across four independent military cohorts found that mindfulness training preserved baseline attention and emotional resilience under extreme operational stress — it did not improve performance above baseline. The goal of this framework is to maintain monitoring capacity under load, not to become superhuman under pressure. A minimum of ~11 minutes/day of practice maintains this protective effect; below that, trained capacities erode under stress exposure. PFC-dependent deliberate strategies (Raio et al., 2013) require automaticity to be stress-resistant — effortful regulation provides no protection when noradrenergic load is high.

## Measurement Tracks

### Track A — Subjective / Behavioral

| Instrument | What It Measures | Used In Phase |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Session quality log | Practice consistency, engagement, self-observed events | All |
| Ecological momentary assessment (EMA) | Real-time internal state across daily contexts | P1–P5 |
| Emotion log with source attribution | E1, E2 domain skills | P2–P4 |
| Thought/assumption journal | C2 domain skills | P3–P4 |
| Pattern library document | P3 domain skills | P5–P8 |
| FFMQ — Observing subscale | Meta-attention proxy (A2.1, A2.2) | P3, P5, gate, P8 |
| SMS-Mind subscale | Thought observation, narrative monitoring (C2.1, C2.6) | P3, P4, gate, P8 |
| PHQ-9 / DASS-21 | Emotional regulation, distress reduction (indirect) | P2, P4, P5 |
| Rumination Response Scale | Narrative monitoring quality (C2.6 indirect) | P4, gate |
| Self-calibration comparison | I1.3 — calibration honesty | All phase gates |

### Track B — Physiological / Performance

| Instrument | What It Measures | Used In Phase |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Heartbeat detection task (Schandry-type) | Interoceptive accuracy (S1.2) | P1 |
| Resting pulse vs. subjective estimate | Interoceptive accuracy crosscheck (S1.2) | P1–P2 |
| Arousal rating vs. objective marker (HR, breath rate) | Arousal level detection (S1.4) | P1–P2 |
| Recovery time measurement (post-disruption) | Recovery speed (I1.2) | P5–P8 |
| Dual-task performance under internal probing | Dual presence without degradation (A2.2) | P5, gate |
| Pre/post arousal measures (relaxation protocol) | Voluntary relaxation (M3.1) | P6 |
| Attention sustaining duration (session log) | A1.1 — objective duration | P1–P2 |
| Ecological prediction accuracy | Pattern recognition (P3.1) | P7–P8 |
| Mild physiological arousal induction + skill assessment | I1.1 quality under noradrenergic load (post-exercise or cold exposure), not only under cognitive/emotional load | P5, L2→L3 gate |

**Key constraint:** A gate cannot be passed on Track A evidence alone. Both tracks must show threshold-level performance before progression.

## Phase 0 — Entry Assessment

**Purpose:** Establish individual baseline across all Layer 1 domains. Do not assume any starting level.

**What is measured:**
- Native somatic awareness range (S1.1, S1.2 proxies)
- Emotional vocabulary breadth and granularity (E1.1, E1.3 proxies)
- Native attentional sustaining duration (A1.1 proxy)
- Alexithymia screening (Toronto Alexithymia Scale — TAS-20)
- Self-calibration baseline for I1.3

**Method:**
1. TAS-20 questionnaire
2. 10-minute guided body scan with verbal report (somatic baseline)
3. Emotional labeling exercise: 20 scenario descriptions, identify and name emotional state
4. 10-minute sustained attention session: self-report duration without drift
5. Personal emotional vocabulary audit: list all emotion words known
6. Heartbeat detection task: 25-beat counting across 4 intervals

**Outputs:**
- Assigned entry point (Phase 1 standard, Phase 1 extended, or Phase 1 somatic-priority)
- TAS-20 score on file (baseline; alexithymia affects training pace in Domains 1–2)
- Heartbeat detection accuracy score (baseline interoceptive accuracy)
- Emotional vocabulary size (baseline; < 10 words = somatic-priority track)

**Flags:**
- TAS-20 > 61 (clinically significant alexithymia): flag for extended Phase 1 and extended Phase 2 before progression
- Emotional vocabulary < 10 distinct states: Phase 2 extended
- Heartbeat detection < 60% accuracy: expect 2–4 week extended Phase 1

## Phase 1 — Somatic & Attentional Foundation

**Duration:** 4–8 weeks (extended if alexithymia or very low somatic baseline)

**Primary skills:**
- S1.1 Sensation Recognition
- S1.2 Interoceptive Accuracy
- A1.1 Attention Sustaining
- A1.2 Attention Directing
- A1.3 Attention Recovery
- E1.1 Primary Emotion Identification *(begins here, not complete)*

**Core practices:**
- Body scan (30 min daily): develops S1.1, S1.2. Not breath-focused meditation.
- Sensation labeling exercise (10 min daily): isolate and name single sensations without interpretation
- Focused attention session on single sensation object (15 min daily): A1.1, A1.2, A1.3
- Primary emotion check-in (3× daily, 2 min each): E1.1 begins

**What "sufficient" looks like at Phase 1 exit:**

| Skill | Threshold | Assessment Method |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| S1.1 | Can identify and locate 5+ distinct physical sensations in a 10-minute body scan. Uses physical terms (pressure, heat, tension, vibration) not emotional terms. Consistent across 3 sessions. | Guided body scan with verbal report |
| S1.2 | Heartbeat detection accuracy ≥ 70% (vs. 25-beat intervals). Can detect change in breath depth and gut tension on demand. | Heartbeat detection task (Track B) |
| A1.1 | Can maintain voluntary focus for 10+ continuous minutes with < 3 self-reported unrecovered drifts. Consistent across 5 sessions. | Session log (Track A) + duration record (Track B) |
| A1.2 | Can move attention to a specified body region within 5 seconds. Can correctly identify (>75%) whether attention was chosen or pulled. | Attention direction + discrimination probe |
| A1.3 | Returns to attentional object within 15 seconds of noticing drift. Improving trend over sessions. | Recovery latency log (Track A) |
| E1.1 | Correctly identifies current primary emotional state in > 80% of structured prompts. Can distinguish at least 5 of the 7 primary states. | Emotion identification log, 2-week EMA |

**Phase 1 failure modes:**
- Practicing breath-focused meditation instead of body scan — somatic progress will stall
- Describing sensations in emotional or interpretive terms ("it feels sad") — redirect to physical
- Forcing attentional duration beyond capacity — learn recovery, not endurance
- Skipping E1.1 check-ins because "I know what I feel" — this is a false baseline assumption

## Phase 2 — Emotional Literacy & Attentional Depth

**Duration:** 4–8 weeks (extended if TAS-20 > 61)

**Primary skills:**
- S1.3 Physiological Baseline Mapping
- S1.4 Arousal Level Detection
- E1.2 Somatic-Emotion Mapping
- E1.3 Emotional Vocabulary Expansion
- E1.4 Emotional Gradient Detection
- A1.4 Distraction Resistance
- A1.5 Aperture Control

**Core practices:**
- Daily physiological journal (5 min): document neutral state readings across 4 body systems (S1.3)
- Emotion-body map construction: structured exercise linking each emotion to its body signature (E1.2)
- Vocabulary expansion exercises: emotion synonym trees, granularity distinctions (E1.3)
- Real-time gradient logging (5× daily): catch emotions at low intensity (E1.4)
- Focused attention in varied distraction environments (A1.4)
- Deliberate aperture shifting: narrow → point focus, widen → field (A1.5)

**What "sufficient" looks like at Phase 2 exit:**

| Skill | Threshold | Assessment Method |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| S1.3 | Has a documented personal "neutral" reference across ≥ 4 body systems. Can identify deviation from personal neutral reliably across different times of day. Documented over 2 weeks. | Daily physiology journal reviewed |
| S1.4 | Can place current arousal on a 0–10 scale with ≤ 2 points error against HR-based estimate. Consistent across 7 days of EMA. | EMA arousal + HR crosscheck (Track B) |
| E1.2 | Has documented a personal emotion-body map covering ≥ 5 emotions with specific body locations and sensation types. Can identify emotional state from somatic cues alone in > 70% of scenarios. | Body map document + somatic-cue identification test |
| E1.3 | Can name and distinguish ≥ 20 distinct emotional states. Can explain functional difference between similar states (dread vs. apprehension vs. anxiety). | Emotion differentiation vocabulary audit |
| E1.4 | Can detect and report onset of an emotional state at intensity ≤ 3/10 in > 60% of EMA prompts. Real-time vs. retrospective reporting gap < 2 intensity points. | EMA real-time vs. retrospective comparison (Track A+B) |
| A1.4 | Can maintain attentional focus for 5 minutes with < 2 unrecovered drifts in the presence of ambient distraction. Tested in ≥ 3 distinct distraction environments. | Ecological attention log (Track A) |
| A1.5 | Can deliberately narrow to point-focus and widen to field awareness on request. Can sustain each state for 60 seconds. Transition time < 30 seconds. | Guided practice with verbal state report |

**Phase 2 failure modes:**
- Building a generic emotion-body map ("anxiety lives in the chest") rather than a personal one. Must be derived from personal experience, not knowledge.
- Not logging gradient detections because there's "nothing to notice" — this means the threshold is too high, not that it's absent
- Training aperture control only in calm settings — must be practiced under mild distraction

## Phase 3 — Layer 1→2 Bridge

**Duration:** 4–8 weeks

**Purpose:** Begin Layer 2 skills while Layer 1 is still consolidating. Introduces meta-awareness and cognitive observation without yet requiring full Layer 2 performance. I1.3 (Calibration Honesty) is formally introduced here.

**Primary skills:**
- A2.1 Meta-Attention *(begins here)*
- C2.1 Thought Observation *(begins here)*
- C2.2 Cognitive State Labeling *(begins here)*
- I1.3 Calibration Honesty *(introduced here; first formal self-assessment)*

**Core practices:**
- Meta-attention probes: random prompts during normal activity — "Where is my attention right now?" (A2.1)
- Thought observation sits (20 min daily): watch thoughts arise without identification (C2.1)
- Cognitive mode journaling: label the mode of thinking you were just in, 5× daily (C2.2)
- Formal Phase 3 self-assessment followed by comparison against criterion (I1.3)

**What "sufficient" looks like at Phase 3 exit:**

| Skill | Threshold | Assessment Method |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| A2.1 | Can accurately report current attentional object in ≥ 80% of random probes, without redirecting attention. Latency to meta-awareness < 10 seconds. | Random probe log (Track A); blind-scored against actual attentional context |
| C2.1 | Can complete a 20-minute session reporting thoughts as events ("I had the thought…") > 80% of the time. Can catch identification in the moment (not only retrospectively) in > 5 instances per week. | Session log; SMS-Mind subscale at baseline and Phase 3 end |
| C2.2 | Can correctly label current cognitive mode in ≥ 70% of structured prompts. Can distinguish ≥ 5 distinct modes. Mode journal shows entries across > 10 days. | Cognitive mode journal + structured labeling test |
| I1.3 (provisional) | Self-assessed skill levels are within ± 1 point of criterion assessment for ≥ 70% of Phase 1–2 skills reviewed. No systematic direction bias (not consistently over or under). | Blind criterion assessment vs. self-report |

**Phase 3 failure modes:**
- Using thought observation sessions to suppress thoughts rather than watch them — the goal is non-interference
- Cognitive mode labeling without specificity ("I was thinking") — must identify mode, not just acknowledge thinking
- Calibration honesty check showing systematic overestimation — flag this prominently; do not advance to Phase 4 without addressing the bias pattern

## Phase 4 — Metacognitive Core

**Duration:** 8–12 weeks

**Primary skills:**
- C2.3 Process Awareness
- C2.4 Cognitive Load Sensing
- C2.5 Assumption Detection
- C2.6 Narrative Monitoring
- E2.1 Emotional Source Attribution
- E2.2 Emotional Contagion Awareness
- E2.3 Affect-Cognition Coupling Detection

**Core practices:**
- Process awareness journaling (daily): "How was I thinking?" not "What did I think?" (C2.3)
- Assumption log (daily): identify 1 inference being treated as observation (C2.5)
- Narrative catch practice: real-time — "I am telling myself a story about X" (C2.6)
- Cognitive load check before and after demanding activities (C2.4)
- Emotion source log: after every significant emotion, attribute source (E2.1)
- Contagion audit: 1× weekly review of environments and interactions for emotional state shifts (E2.2)
- Decision review: 3× weekly, identify where emotion shaped a conclusion (E2.3)

**What "sufficient" looks like at Phase 4 exit:**

| Skill | Threshold | Assessment Method |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| C2.3 | Can identify 3+ reasoning shortcuts or collapsed inferences per week in own thinking, documented in journal for ≥ 4 consecutive weeks. Can name the type of shortcut (overgeneralization, availability bias, etc.). | Assumption/process journal reviewed |
| C2.4 | Can accurately rate current cognitive capacity (1–5) with ≤ 1 point error against a task-based performance proxy, across ≥ 10 paired assessments. | Self-rating + performance proxy comparison (Track B) |
| C2.5 | Can tag ≥ 3 assumptions per week (distinct from C2.3) where inference was treated as direct observation. Can explain the distinction from the real-time moment, not only in retrospect. | Assumption log with timing metadata |
| C2.6 | Can catch a running narrative in real time in > 5 instances per week (documented). Can label the narrative theme. Rumination Response Scale shows measurable decrease from Phase 0 baseline. | Narrative log; Rumination Response Scale pre/post |
| E2.1 | For ≥ 70% of logged emotional states, can provide a reasoned source attribution within 60 seconds. Attribution reasoning documented. | Emotion source log reviewed |
| E2.2 | Documents ≥ 2 contagion instances per week. Can correctly anticipate high-contagion contexts (predictive accuracy > 50%). | Contagion log + predictive accuracy check |
| E2.3 | Can identify affect-cognition coupling in real time (before the biased conclusion is finalized) in ≥ 3 instances per week. Can name the coupling pattern. | Decision review log; real-time flag rate |

**Phase 4 failure modes:**
- Process awareness journaling that describes content ("I thought about work") rather than process ("I was catastrophizing about future outcomes") — consistently redirect to the *how*
- Narrative monitoring that only catches narratives in hindsight — real-time catch is required
- Emotional source attribution that is always either "internal" or "external" without variation — this signals pattern, not genuine assessment

## Phase 5 — Self-Boundary & Dual Presence

**Duration:** 8–12 weeks

**Primary skills:**
- B2.1 Self-Other Signal Distinction
- B2.2 Incongruence Detection
- B2.3 Value Anchoring Under Pressure
- B2.4 Self-Attribution Consistency Monitoring
- A2.2 Dual Presence *(fully functional here)*
- I1.1 Skill-Under-Load *(begins here)*
- I1.2 Recovery Speed *(begins tracking here)*

**Core practices:**
- Values document construction and regular reference (B2.2, B2.3)
- Self-description baseline document: establish and date it (B2.4)
- Weekly baseline review: compare current self-description against baseline (B2.4)
- Structured challenge scenarios (media exposure, social pressure situations): real-time B2.1 practice
- Dual presence sessions: routine activity (reading, conversation) with concurrent internal state monitoring (A2.2)
- Load-condition practice: perform monitoring skills during cognitive or emotional load (I1.1 begins)
- Recovery time logging: measure return to baseline after disruptions (I1.2 begins)

**What "sufficient" looks like at Phase 5 exit:**

| Skill | Threshold | Assessment Method |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| B2.1 | Can distinguish internally-generated from externally-introduced cognitive/emotional content with > 70% accuracy in structured scenarios. Can describe distinguishing features used. | Structured challenge scenarios with immediate attribution (Track A) |
| B2.2 | Can identify when a thought or impulse is inconsistent with stated core values within 60 seconds of noticing, in > 80% of structured probes. Values document in place and current. | Values-consistency probes; values document review |
| B2.3 | Can return to values-alignment within 2 minutes of a cognitive load or social pressure challenge (S1.4 arousal ≤ 6/10). Consistent across ≥ 5 structured scenarios. Does not require external prompt. | Pressure scenario protocol (Track A+B) |
| B2.4 | Has a baseline self-description document, dated and signed. Can identify meaningful drift within 1 week of onset. (*Drift = self-description change that occurred without conscious noticing and endorsement — adaptive or maladaptive.*) Weekly review entries in place for ≥ 8 consecutive weeks. Drift detection latency decreasing over time. | Longitudinal self-description + weekly review log |
| A2.2 | Can maintain background internal state monitoring during a 20-minute cognitive task. Can report accurately on internal state when probed, without task performance dropping below 85% of baseline on the same task. | Dual-task protocol with internal probe and performance measure (Track B) |
| I1.1 (provisional) | Can maintain A2.1 (meta-attention), A2.2 (dual presence), C2.1 (thought observation), C2.6 (narrative monitoring), B2.1 (self-other distinction), and B2.3 (values anchoring) at ≥ 80% of rested quality under mild-to-moderate load. Tested across ≥ 2 distinct load types. | Load-condition skill assessment (Track A+B) — all 6 skills assessed |
| I1.2 (provisional) | Returns to monitoring baseline within 15 minutes of documented disruption. Baseline quality ≥ 85% of pre-disruption level. Tracking trend established. | Post-disruption recovery log (Track B) |

**Phase 5 failure modes:**
- Values document is vague or aspirational rather than behavioral and specific — must describe observable patterns, not ideals
- Dual presence practice only in structured sessions — must transfer to daily activity
- B2.1 showing only binary attribution ("internal" or "external") without gradation or uncertainty marking
- Skipping weekly baseline reviews for B2.4 — this skill only exists with longitudinal data

## Phase 6 — Layer 2 Verification

**Duration:** 2–4 weeks

**Purpose:** Not new skill acquisition — stability verification. Confirms Layer 2 skills function under realistic conditions before Layer 3 access. This is the gate that protects the integrity of the framework.

**No new skills introduced in this phase.**

**Verification activities:**
- All Phase 4 and Phase 5 skills retested under mild load condition
- I1.1 (Skill-Under-Load) tested across 3 distinct load conditions
- I1.3 (Calibration Honesty) full re-assessment: compare self-ratings against criterion for all L1 and L2 skills
- B2.4 drift review: look for any identity drift during the preceding 12 weeks
- A2.2 (Dual Presence) tested in ecologically valid contexts (normal work/social activity), not controlled sessions

## L2 → L3 Gate (Critical)

This is the highest-stakes boundary in the framework. Layer 3 involves deliberate modification of patterns you can only safely change if you can perceive them accurately and continuously. Premature entry is the primary serious failure mode.

**All of the following must be demonstrated before Phase 7 begins:**

### Mandatory Gate Criteria

| Criterion | Evidence Required |
|-----------|-------------------|
| All L2 skills at threshold | Phase 4 and Phase 5 exit criteria met. No unmet skill. |
| I1.1 at threshold across 3 load conditions | Different types of load: cognitive, emotional, social. All ≥ 80% of rested baseline. |
| I1.3 accurate (no systematic bias) | Self-assessment within ±1 point for ≥ 80% of assessed skills, no direction bias. |
| B2.1–B2.4 functional under pressure | Not just in quiet practice. Must be demonstrated in realistic challenge conditions. |
| A2.2 dual presence stable for ≥ 6 weeks | Session log shows consistent ecologically valid dual presence. |
| Track B progress confirmed independently | At least 2 Track B measures show threshold-level performance, independent of Track A self-report. |
| Written self-assessment completed | Practitioner writes a self-evaluation of Layer 2 competency. Compared against criterion. |
| PHQ-9 ≤ 9 (mild or below) | If ≥ 10, resolve before entering Layer 3. Elevated depressive symptoms impair the perceptual accuracy Layer 3 requires. |
| No active crisis indicators | Sleep disruption, flashbacks, and dissociative episodes must all be absent or resolved. Layer 3 self-modification requires stable baseline function. |
| I1.1 demonstrated under physiological arousal | At least one I1.1 assessment must be completed immediately after mild physiological arousal induction (brief vigorous exercise or cold exposure). Cognitive and emotional load testing alone is insufficient — noradrenergic stress specifically degrades PFC-dependent regulation via a mechanism that bypasses motivation and effort (Raio et al., 2013). Skills must be tested at the level where the firewall actually operates. |

### Gate Failure Conditions

If any of the following are present, do not advance:
- I1.3 showing systematic overestimation (person believes they are more capable than they are)
- B2.4 showing undetected drift over the preceding 12 weeks
- I1.1 below threshold in any of the 3 load conditions tested
- Track A and Track B measures substantially discordant with no explanation
- The practitioner cannot identify their own current failure modes

> **Note:** This gate is not a punishment — it is the point where the framework has actual protective value. A person who enters Layer 3 without meeting this gate is undertaking self-modification they cannot reliably observe. The research basis for this: "You cannot safely change what you cannot perceive" and "the user becomes their own firewall" require that the firewall is actually functional first.

## Phase 7 — State Modulation

**Duration:** 8–12 weeks

**Primary skills:**
- M3.1 Voluntary Relaxation
- M3.2 State Shifting
- M3.3 Arousal Calibration
- M3.4 Emotional Response Modification
- I1.4 Practice Quality Monitoring *(introduced here; mandatory for Layer 3 work)*

**Core practices:**
- Deliberate relaxation protocol (20 min daily, varied methods): M3.1
- State shifting exercises: define 3 target states, practice transitions between them (M3.2)
- Pre-task arousal calibration ritual (M3.3): before demanding tasks, consciously set arousal level
- Emotion induction + modulation practice: M3.4 (not suppression — deliberate adjustment with awareness)
- I1.4 session log: rate each session against 4 quality criteria

**What "sufficient" looks like at Phase 7 exit:**

| Skill | Threshold | Assessment Method |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| M3.1 | Can reduce subjective arousal by ≥ 2 points (0–10 scale) within 5 minutes using a deliberate method. HR shows measurable reduction (≥ 5 bpm). Consistent across ≥ 8 sessions in varied contexts. | Arousal rating + HR pre/post (Track B) |
| M3.2 | Can shift between 3 defined states on demand within 5 minutes. Can sustain each state for 10 minutes. Can accurately report current state in real time. | State shifting protocol with pre/post rating and verbal report |
| M3.3 | Can calibrate pre-task arousal to stated target level, with ≤ 2 points error on 0–10 scale, across ≥ 10 pre-task calibrations spanning diverse task types. | Pre-task calibration log + error rate (Track B) |
| M3.4 | Can reduce emotional intensity by ≥ 2 points (0–10) within 5 minutes after detection and attribution. Somatic signature remains detectable (not suppressed). Consistent across ≥ 5 emotion types. | Emotion induction + modulation protocol (Track A+B) |
| I1.4 | Can assess each practice session against 4 quality criteria (behavioral relevance, appropriate challenge, contextual variety, intentional engagement). Has identified and adjusted at least 1 quality dimension per week for ≥ 6 consecutive weeks. | Session quality log reviewed |

**Phase 7 failure modes:**
- Voluntary relaxation that works only in ideal conditions — must be tested across varied contexts
- State shifting that produces the target label without the target state — Track B physiological confirmation required
- Emotional response modification becoming suppression — the somatic signature should still be present and detectable
- I1.4 log that is consistently "high quality" without any adjustments — signals non-genuine assessment

## Phase 8 — Pattern Work

**Duration:** Ongoing (no ceiling)

**Primary skills:**
- P3.1 Personal Pattern Recognition
- P3.2 Pattern Interruption
- P3.3 Deliberate Reframing
- P3.4 Topology Mapping
- P3.5 Directed Neuroplastic Practice
- I1.1 Skill-Under-Load *(full threshold achieved here)*
- I1.2 Recovery Speed *(full threshold achieved here)*

**Core practices:**
- Pattern documentation: actively log recurring cognitive-emotional patterns as they are detected (P3.1)
- Interruption attempts: for each identified pattern, set an intentional interruption condition (P3.2)
- Reframing exercises: generate 3+ alternative framings for recurring meaning structures (P3.3)
- Topology map construction: connect patterns into a structural model; update ≥ monthly (P3.4)
- Directed practice cycles: select a target from topology map, design and execute a deliberate reshaping practice over 6+ weeks (P3.5)
- I1.4 quality monitoring: mandatory for P3.5 — every directed practice session assessed for quality conditions

**What "sufficient" looks like (Phase 8 checkpoints at 12 and 24 weeks):**

| Skill | Threshold | Assessment Method |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| P3.1 | Has documented ≥ 5 recurring cognitive-emotional patterns with triggers, response signatures, and context conditions. Can predict own response to trigger situations with > 60% accuracy. | Pattern library document reviewed; predictive accuracy log (Track A) |
| P3.2 | Can interrupt ≥ 3 identified patterns before completion across 2 consecutive weeks. Success rate > 50% on targeted patterns. Can distinguish successful interruption from pattern suppression. | Pattern interruption log with outcome tracking (Track A+B) |
| P3.3 | Can produce ≥ 3 alternative framings of the same event or belief on demand. Can maintain awareness of doing so (not confusing reframe with objective reality). Can identify when a reframe is self-deception rather than genuine reframing. | Reframing exercise + reflection log; self-deception flag rate |
| P3.4 | Has produced a documented topology map with ≥ 10 connected patterns, beliefs, and trigger points. Map is actively used for P3.2 and P3.3 decisions. Map has been updated ≥ 3 times. | Topology document with version history |
| P3.5 | Has completed ≥ 1 full directed practice cycle: target identified from topology map → deliberate practice protocol designed → executed consistently for ≥ 6 weeks → measurable change in target pattern documented → quality conditions tracked throughout via I1.4. | Practice cycle documentation; pre/post pattern measure (Track A+B) |
| I1.1 (full) | All Layer 2 skills maintained at ≥ 80% of rested performance across ≥ 3 distinct load types (cognitive, emotional, social), sustained over ≥ 4 weeks of ecologically valid conditions. | Load-condition skill assessments (Track A+B) |
| I1.2 (full) | Returns to monitoring baseline within 10 minutes of disruption. Baseline quality ≥ 90% of pre-disruption level. Decreasing recovery time trend visible over 12 weeks. | Post-disruption recovery log (Track B) |

**Phase 8 failure modes:**
- Pattern interruption becoming pattern suppression — if somatic signature disappears without awareness, it's suppression
- Topology map that is descriptive but not used operationally — must drive P3.2 and P3.3 decisions
- Directed practice without quality monitoring (I1.4 decoupled from P3.5) — quantity of practice without quality = no plasticity
- Deliberate reframing that produces comfort rather than accuracy — validate reframes against B2.2 (incongruence detection)

## Cross-Layer Skill Thresholds

### I1.1 — Skill-Under-Load

| Stage | Threshold | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Provisional (Phase 5) | ≥ 80% of rested quality for A2.1, C2.1, B2.1 under mild-to-moderate load, ≥ 2 conditions | Phase 5 |
| L2→L3 gate | ≥ 80% across all Layer 2 monitoring skills, ≥ 3 load types including at least one physiological arousal condition (post-exercise or cold exposure) | Phase 6 gate |
| Full (Phase 8) | ≥ 80% across all Layer 2, ≥ 3 load types, ecologically valid, sustained 4 weeks | Phase 8 12-week checkpoint |

### I1.2 — Recovery Speed

| Stage | Threshold | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Provisional (Phase 5) | Returns to baseline within 15 min, ≥ 85% baseline quality | Phase 5 |
| Full (Phase 8) | Returns to baseline within 10 min, ≥ 90% baseline quality, decreasing trend | Phase 8 12-week checkpoint |

### I1.3 — Calibration Honesty

## Failure Mode Catalog

| Failure Mode | Where It Appears | Consequence | Detection |
|---|---|---|---|
| Premature L3 entry | L2→L3 gate | Cannot perceive what is being modified; risk of non-reversible pattern change | Gate criteria explicitly block this; I1.3 check |
| Calibration overconfidence (I1.3 systematic overestimation) | Any gate, Phase 3+ | False sense of readiness; bypasses safety margins | Criterion vs. self-report comparison |
| Somatic practice replaced by breath meditation | Phase 1, 2 | S1.1–S1.4 stall; downstream skills never fully develop | Track B: heartbeat detection not improving |
| Thought observation used for thought suppression | Phase 3, 4 | SMS-Mind scores may look adequate; actual suppression increases distress | PHQ-9 / DASS-21 increasing vs. decreasing |
| Attention training only in quiet settings | Phase 1–3 | I1.1 will fail under load; skills remain context-specific | Load-condition failure at Phase 5 |
| Narrative monitoring only in retrospect | Phase 4 | Real-time protection absent; influence completes before detection | Session log shows only post-hoc catches |
| Pattern interruption → pattern suppression | Phase 8 | Somatic signal masked; pattern operating covertly | Track B: somatic signature disappears without awareness change |
| I1.4 logged without genuine assessment | Phase 7–8 | No plasticity from practice despite hours invested | All sessions rated "high quality"; no adjustments recorded |
| B2.4 skipped or superficial | Phase 5+ | Identity drift detectable only after significant displacement | Longitudinal self-description comparison shows large gaps |
| Track A and Track B discordance (unexplained) | Any gate | One of the tracks is unreliable; masks true skill state | Gate requires both tracks at threshold |
| Stress-transfer failure | L2→L3 gate; Phase 7–8 | PFC-dependent regulation skills trained only under cognitive or emotional load produce zero effect under acute physiological stress (noradrenergic mechanism, not motivation). The person believes they have the skill but it is not available in exactly the conditions where it is needed most | I1.1 physiological arousal assessment (Track B) — skills that fail here are not ready for Layer 3 |
| Metacognitive unavailability under active WM load | Phase 4–8; I1.1 assessment | Active working-memory engagement — including rumination, worry, and planning loops — specifically degrades self-monitoring sensitivity. A person can accurately observe thoughts in quiet practice but lose that capacity entirely when internally preoccupied. This is distinct from skill degradation under external stress | Dual-task I1.1 probe using active mental generation (not just external distraction); Track B |

## Timescale Reference

Based on convergent research findings (see `research-grounding.md`):

| Timescale | Expected Changes | Research Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | Subjective experience changes, self-report improvements, reduced distress | MBSR/MBCT outcome literature |
| Weeks 4–8 | Functional connectivity reconfiguration; network efficiency gains; may be independent of self-report | Yue et al. (2023), Ganesan et al. (2025) |
| Weeks 8+ | Structural changes: cortical thickness, hippocampal volume | Calderone et al. (2024) |
| Months 3–12 | Pattern stability, relapse prevention maintenance, identity drift stabilization | Gkintoni et al. (2025) — 60-week follow-up data |
| Years | Expert effects: sustained and increasing (not habituating) prefrontal activation | Calderone et al. (2024), Davanger et al. cited |

> These timescales describe when changes become detectable, not when they are complete. Do not interpret 8-week thresholds as endpoints.
